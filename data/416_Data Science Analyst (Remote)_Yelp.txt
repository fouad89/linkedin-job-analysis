Data Science Analyst (Remote)
Yelp
Aberdeen, Scotland, United Kingdom
At Yelp, it’s our mission to connect people with great local businesses. From a coffee connoisseur’s review of a neighborhood cafe, to a homeowner’s request for a quote to remodel their kitchen, Yelp’s data captures millions of user-business interactions every day.

The Consumer Data Science & Analytics team focuses on the people side of these interactions, harnessing the power of this data to understand and optimize Yelp’s user experience. We design and run experiments, build tools, and lead meaningful analyses that influence Yelp’s consumer product and business strategy.

We’d love to have you apply, even if you don't feel you meet every single requirement in this posting. At Yelp, we’re looking for great people, not just those who simply check off all the boxes.

Where You Come In
Lead broad analytics projects core to Yelp’s business, communicate key insights to decision makers, and help product leaders uncover new opportunities
Manage tight deadlines and quickly iterate analyses to answer unstructured business questions, deliver actionable learnings, and make strategic recommendations
Work cross-functionally with product and engineering to define, log, and track Yelp’s key product metrics and opportunity size new initiatives
Generate dashboards and automate reports that empower teams across the organization to make data-driven decisions
Grow and maintain impactful relationships with stakeholders of both technical and non-technical backgrounds with clear and frequent communication
Support consumer experimentation from initial design to synthesizing learnings from experimental results
What It Takes to Succeed
Based or willing to relocate within the United Kingdom
Critical thinker, team player, and excellent communicator who takes pride in telling stories with data
A knack for communicating quantitative results to a broad audience through writing and compelling data visualization
BA/BS or graduate degree in a quantitative field (mathematics, statistics, economics, computer science, etc.)
Several years of quantitative industry experience, ideally within the consumer product space
Excellent SQL skills, fluency in Python, and experience with at least one statistical package (R, Pandas/NumPy/SciPy/statsmodels)
Extensive experience with at least one data visualization tool (Tableau, Power BI, Mode, Looker)
Solid understanding of statistical inference and experimental design
Familiarity with financial modeling and advanced statistics (regression, non parametrics, predictive modeling)
