Data Scientist - Anti-Financial Crime (AFC)
Deutsche Bank
Birmingham, England, United Kingdom
Job Title: Anti-Financial Crime (AFC) Data Scientist
Location: Birmingham
Corporate Title: Assistant Vice President
 You will be joining the AFC Modelling team, which is responsible for developing and optimising methods and models to monitor clients’ transactional behaviour that aligns with the Bank’s risk appetite, identifying systemic transactional threats to proactively combat financial crime matters, and support Financial Crime Investigation team from data and analytic needs.

Your key responsibilities
Responsible for supporting model development within the AFC analytics programme including: monitoring rule design, development, segmentation, risk assessment and validation within the team
Responsible for performing proactive risk assessments using subject matter expertise, industry trends, emerging AFC risks
Work with the Financial Crime Operations Team and the Risk Coverage / Governance Team to ensure the Transaction Monitoring rules are reviewed on a regular basis based on the level of risk and that the systems are appropriately optimised
Conduct Anti-Money Laundering (AML) tuning and optimisation across multiple line of business’s (including correspondent banking, retail, commercial, brokerage, and trade finance) and multiple Transaction Monitoring Systems (Mantas and Smaragd), ensure sound risk coverage, adequate quantitative model assessment and validation, and data quality completeness and integrity
Support the development and maintenance of bespoke Threat Analytics models targeting specific money laundering risks
Your skills and experience
Excellent knowledge of AML Transaction Monitoring systems particularly Oracle Mantas
Demonstrated experience in analytics platforms (Big Data, Scala, PySpark, Python, R, etc.) for modelling, data mining and statistical analysis
Demonstrably strong knowledge of SQL
Good experience in Financial Crime Compliance, Risk Assessment or Quality Assurance in Financial Services, Banking or Regulatory environment
Experience in dealing with internal and external audit
